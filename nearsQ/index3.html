GUIDELINES

Stay informed: Monitor official alerts and updates regularly. Medical considerations: Have medications and medical records accessible. Follow authorities: Adhere to evacuation orders and instructions.

Earthquake

Flood

Fire

Typhoon

  1. Drop, Cover, and Hold On: Take cover under a sturdy piece of furniture, and hold on until shaking stops
  2. Stay indoors: Avoid running outside during the shaking to minimize the risk of falling debris
  3. Stay away from windows: Move away from glass windows to prevent injury from shattered glass

Before Flood

  1. Create an emergency kit with essentials like water, non-perishable food, medications, and important documents.
  2. Install flood barriers and check the condition of your home's foundation and drainage systems.
  3. Elevate valuables and electrical appliances in your home.

During Flood

  1. Evacuate to higher ground if advised by authorities
  2. Seek refuge in a sturdy building and avoid contact with floodwater, which may be contaminated
  3. Avoid walking or driving through flooded areas, as water levels can be deceptive and pose dangers.

After Flood

  1. Wait for authorities to declare it safe before returning home.
  2. Seek refuge in a sturdy building and avoid contact with floodwater, which may be contaminated
  3. Seek professional help for repairs and follow any recommendations from emergency services.
  1. Immediate Evacuation:
    Leave the area promptly using designated escape routes, avoiding elevators. Stay low if there is smoke.
  2. Alert Others:
    Notify others by shouting or activating fire alarms.Assist those requiring help in evacuating.
  3. No Re-entry Until Cleared:
    Do not re-enter until authorities declare the area safe. Follow emergency personnel instructions.
  1. Stay Informed:
    Monitor weather forecasts and official alerts regularly. Know the evacuation routes and nearest shelters.
  2. Prepare Emergency Kit:
    Assemble a typhoon preparedness kit with essentials like water, non-perishable food, first aid supplies, flashlight, and important documents.
  3. Secure Property:
    Reinforce windows and doors. Trim trees and secure loose outdoor items.
